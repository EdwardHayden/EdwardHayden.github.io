Runeguard 2

2022

Background info

Having spent 2021 creating the prototype for a joint-stamina turn-based mechanic, I wanted to go back through the original project, incorperate more gameplay features, and refine the features already there. I spent my free time in 2022 remaking Runeguard from the ground up now that I had experience in Godot. By the end of the year, I managed to turn a mechanic prototype into a cleaned up, working game demo with several new features and a simple gameplay loop.

About the game

Runeguard 2 builds on the original prototype by:

  • Refining the 'joint-stamina turn-based mechanic (along with the UI)
  • Re-designing the map
  • Adding a levelling up system that lets you increase stats upon levelling up
  • Adding a lootable chest to unlock a new weapon
  • Adding a simple inventory system to swap between an axe, a sword, and magic, all with their own stats
  • Adding an objective to the level in the form of a lever that needs to be pulled to progress
  • Adding a second smaller level with a more powerful enemy as a bossfight
  • Adding a win and lose screen that shows your score

Skills Gained

With this being my second attempt at making a game in Godot, I used it as a chance to reinforce the skills I gained in my previous Runeguard project whilst also trying out creating new systems like an inventory and lootable chests. As I put more focus on this project into the actual gameplay, I was also able to put more thought into the design of the game in areas such as the new map and placement of assets to lead the player and present a goal.

  • Reinforced Knowledge of Godot Game Engine
  • Reinforced Simple Games Programming Techniques
  • Simple Game Mechanics
  • Simple Level Design
  • Simple UI Programming and Design
